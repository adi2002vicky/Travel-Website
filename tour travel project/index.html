Story Of Ayodhya

Ayodhya is birthplace of Lord Rama venerable of Hindus. He was son of Raja Dashrath of ikshvaku dynasty. Ayodhya was established by King Manu in 6673 B.C. which is currently in Uttar Pradesh state of India. It is one of the seven Holly religious places of Hindus including Ayodhya,Mathura ,Haridwar ,Kashi ,Kanchi Avantika and Dwarka. Durng Buddha Kingdom, it was capital of kosala province. Out of 24 Tirthankars of holy jain dharma 22 were from in ikshvaku dynasty. In 1528 Mir Baqi , Army commander of Babar destroyed the Ram temple and built a Masjid. After 500 years of uphill struggle and by the order of Honorable Supreme Court of India on 5th August 2020 , Prime Minister Narendra Modi put the foundation stone of grand Shriram Temple. The Bhoomi Pujan for the Ram Mandir was attended by around 195 eminent personalities.

Varanasi

I believe you meant "Varanasi." Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and holds great religious and cultural significance. Varanasi is renowned as a major center of spirituality, attracting pilgrims and seekers from all over the world. The city is situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River and is believed to be the abode of Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities in Hinduism. The city is dotted with numerous ancient temples, ghats (riverfront steps), and ashrams. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the most prominent and revered temples in Varanasi. It is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (sacred abodes of Lord Shiva) and is visited by millions of devotees each year. Varanasi is also known for its mesmerizing evening Ganga Aarti (ritual of worship) performed on the banks of the Ganges River.
